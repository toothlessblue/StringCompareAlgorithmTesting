<p><i><b>Waterfall</b></i> is the debut studio album, and the second <a href="/wiki/Charity_album" class="mw-redirect" title="Charity album">charity</a> album by South Korean rapper <a href="/wiki/B.I_(rapper)" title="B.I (rapper)">B.I</a>. It was released on June 1, 2021, by IOK Music X 131 Label, and distributed by <a href="/wiki/Dreamus" title="Dreamus">Dreamus</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The album contains 12 songs including the lead single "Illa Illa", all written and co-produced by B.I alongside various producers such as <a href="/wiki/YG_Entertainment" title="YG Entertainment">YG</a> famous producer <a href="/wiki/Choice37" title="Choice37">Choice37.</a><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <i>the album include</i> two collaborations: "Stay" featuring <a href="/wiki/Tablo" title="Tablo">Tablo</a> and "Long Dream(daydream)" featuring <a href="/wiki/Lee_Hi" title="Lee Hi">Lee Hi</a>. <i>Waterfall</i> tells a condensed story of B.I's year-and-a-half-long hiatus from his perspective.<sup id="cite_ref-nmereview_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nmereview-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Waterfall</i> is B.I's second donation project, the first being <i><a href="/wiki/Midnight_Blue_(Love_Streaming)" title="Midnight Blue (Love Streaming)">Midnight Blue (Love Streaming)</a></i>, with all of the copyright fees, music and album sales, and content revenue generated after the album's release being donated to the emergency relief of "Basic for Girls", conducted by the International Relief Development (NGO) <a href="/wiki/World_Vision_International" title="World Vision International">World Vision</a>. The "Basic for Girls" project aims to improve the poor environment, such as the construction of women's restrooms for women's human rights in Zambia, Africa, and support for sanitary napkins.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Waterfall_(B.I_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On May 7, 2021, 131 Label announced the release schedule for the highly anticipated comeback of B.I as a solo artist, 7 years after debuting as the leader of <a href="/wiki/IKon" title="IKon">iKon</a>. This included a global single, "Got It Like That", featuring <a href="/wiki/Destiny_Rogers" title="Destiny Rogers">Destiny Rogers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tyla_Yaweh" title="Tyla Yaweh">Tyla Yaweh</a> (released on May 14, 2021), and his first full-length album, <i>Waterfall</i> (released on June 1, 2021).<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The 12-track full-length record, <i>Waterfall</i>, is written and composed by B.I, with collaborations from other producers on compositions and arrangements. The album features K-pop vocalist <a href="/wiki/Lee_Hi" title="Lee Hi">Lee Hi</a> (on "Daydream") and rapper <a href="/wiki/Tablo" title="Tablo">Tablo</a> of <a href="/wiki/Epik_High" title="Epik High">Epik High</a> (on "Stay"). A cinematic music video for the title track "illa illa" was also released alongside the album.<sup id="cite_ref-nme_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nme-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>"I wrote the song wishing that it could provide comfort to those that listen to it. Everyone has their own struggles whether it be with relationships, work, family, loneliness," B.I stated of "Illa Illa" in a press release. "As much as it can seem difficult at the time, there is a beach in the distance. To remember that every chapter that closes just means that a new chapter is about to begin."<sup id="cite_ref-nme_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nme-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>"This album is an expression of my past, my present and my future. That every ending has a new beginning," said B.I speaking about <i>Waterfall</i> in an interview.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release_and_promotion">Release and promotion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Waterfall_(B.I_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Release and promotion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>All the promotions before the release were handled through the social media accounts of 131 Label and IOK Music. On May 17, 2021, at midnight KST, B.I's team released a detailed "plan poster" that outlined what fans can expect in the weeks leading up to his comeback.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> This included a live film and performance film for the intro track of the album titled "Waterfall" and short video teaser films for all the other 10 tracks in the album, excluding the title track. In addition, title and concept posters, a lyric mood film, and an M/V spoiler commentary video were released for the title track "illa illa". B.I then held a live broadcast via <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>, 1 hour prior to the new album release.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The album was released to <a href="/wiki/Music_download" title="Music download">digital music</a> and <a href="/wiki/Music_streaming" class="mw-redirect" title="Music streaming">streaming</a> platforms on June 1, 2021,<sup id="cite_ref-hankook_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hankook-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> along with the music video for "illa illa".<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The album's CDs were made available for pre-order prior to the album release, and were released on June 1, 2021 as well. Two different versions of the CDs are available, Waterfall and Seaside.
</p><p>On July 5th, officially imported edition of <i>Waterfall</i> CDs were released in Japan, with official Japanese lyrics. Two different versions of the CDs are available, Waterfall and Seaside.<sup id="cite_ref-cd_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cd-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 11th, 131 Label announced a limited edition <a href="/wiki/Phonograph_record" title="Phonograph record">LP</a>. The pre-order period started on August 13th, 2021 and ended on August 22nd, 2021. The <a href="/wiki/Phonograph_record" title="Phonograph record">LP</a> will be officially released on December 22nd, 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-lp_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lp-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p><i><a href="/wiki/NME" title="NME">NME</a></i> praised the album <i>Waterfall</i>, stating "B.I may have once reached his lowest, but this self-proclaimed waterfall has since been able to smoothen out the ground beneath him and start anew, from creating his own label to making a triumphant return to the music scene with <i>Waterfall</i>. Though he does not claim to be a one-man island; in the songs, there is also an underlying sense of gratitude and appreciation for those who supported and stood by him."<sup id="cite_ref-nmereview_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nmereview-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Time_(magazine)" title="Time (magazine)"><i>Time</i></a> magazine included B.I's title track, "Illa Illa", on its list of "The Best K-Pop Songs of 2021 So Far", stating "with the breadth and depth of emotions he conveys, B.I. shows he's as much a storyteller as he is a songwriter."<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><i>Time</i> also included the album, <i>Waterfall,</i> on its list of "The Best K-Pop Songs and Albums of 2021".<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Waterfall_(B.I_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since its release, <i>Waterfall</i> topped the iTunes album charts in a total of 24 countries including <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>. The album also entered the top 10 list on iTunes chart in the <a href="/wiki/US" class="mw-redirect" title="US">US</a>, while reaching top 3 on the European iTunes album chart and top 2 on the Worldwide iTunes album charts.<sup id="cite_ref-charting_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-charting-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In South Korea, <i>Waterfall</i> debuted at No. 6 on the <a href="/wiki/Gaon_Album_Chart" class="mw-redirect" title="Gaon Album Chart">Gaon Album Chart</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-gaon6_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gaon6-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In Japan, the album debuted at No. 8 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Japan" title="Billboard Japan">Billboard Japan</a> download albums chart, No. 16 on <a href="/wiki/Oricon_Albums_Chart" title="Oricon Albums Chart">Oricon's</a> weekly digital album chart and No. 44 on Oricon's weekly album chart.<sup id="cite_ref-oricon44_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oricon44-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-billboardjp8_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboardjp8-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> <i>Waterfall</i> peaked at No. 1 and achieved certified 3x (Triple) on <a href="/wiki/QQ_Music" title="QQ Music">QQ Music</a>'s best-selling daily album chart in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, selling over 40,000 digital copies.<sup id="cite_ref-charting_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-charting-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Additionally, all 12 tracks of the album, including the title track 'illa illa', were charted-in on the latest 24hit chart of <a href="/wiki/Melon_(online_music_service)" title="Melon (online music service)">Melon</a>, a domestic music platform of <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-charting_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-charting-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The official music video of B.I's title track "Illa Illa" surpassed 12.7 million views on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>, within the first 24 hours of its release. With this, B.I broke the record for the most viewed male solo artist debut M/V within the first 24 hours.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
